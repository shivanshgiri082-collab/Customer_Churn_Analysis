# Customer_Churn_Analysis
# Overview
This project analyzes customer churn patterns across demographics, services, contracts, payments, and usage behavior. The goal is to identify high‑risk segments and propose retention strategies.

# Key Insights

**Demographics**
Age: Right‑skewed; most customers between 25–60 years.
Gender: Females stay longer but also churn more; female new joiners are stronger than males.
Marital Status: Married customers are more stable; non‑married churn more.
State Distribution: Largest bases in Uttar Pradesh and Haryana; smaller bases in Punjab, Puducherry, Karnataka.

**Service Usage**
Phone Service: Customers with phone service churn more.
Internet Service: Majority have internet; higher churn risk among them.
Internet Type: Fiber Optic dominates; DSL moderate, Cable lowest.
Multiple Lines: Balanced between Yes/No; not a strong churn differentiator.
Streaming Services: Balanced adoption for TV/movies; lower for music.
Premium Support: Majority have support, correlating with stability.
Security & Protection: Many lack online security and device protection; backup adoption is stronger.

**Deals & Payments**
Value Deal: Customers with deals churn less; “Unknown” dominates but mostly stayed.
Payment Method: Mailed check users churn more; electronic check and credit card users are steadier.
Paperless Billing: Majority use paperless billing.

**Contracts & Status**

Contract Type: Month‑to‑month dominates → higher churn risk.
Customer Status: Most are “Stayed”; churned second largest; joined smallest.
Referrals: Nearly uniform distribution (0–15) → no strong churn correlation.

**Charges & Revenue**
Tenure: Early churn at 1 month; loyalty improves after 12–24 months.
Monthly Charges: Bimodal — low (~20) vs. high (~80); mid‑tier gap.
Monthly Charge vs. Status: Churned customers have highest median charges → price sensitivity.
Total Charges & Revenue: Right‑skewed; few customers drive most revenue.
Refunds: Majority have none; small group shows dissatisfaction or abuse.
Extra Data Charges: Most near zero; heavy users are upsell + churn risk.

**Churn Reasons**
Categories: “Unknown” dominates; competitor pressure is strong.
Specific Reasons: Competitor offers (better devices, price, coverage, services) drive churn.
Other Issues: Price dissatisfaction, poor service, dropped calls, and customer service complaints appear but at lower counts.

**High‑Risk Segments**
Female customers
Non‑married customers
Internet service users
Mailed‑check payers
Customers without value deals
Month‑to‑month contract holders
High‑charge customers

**Retention Focus**
Target high‑risk groups with tailored offers.
Strengthen loyalty among deal‑holders and electronic payment users.
Promote long‑term contracts to reduce churn.
Improve customer service and competitive positioning.
Protect high‑value customers with loyalty programs and personalized engagement.
